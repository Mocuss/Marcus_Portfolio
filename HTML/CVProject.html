LeafSense

This project was a group project. Our main goal was to create a Computer Vision Model that can identify diseases in leaves.

My role was to improve the model's performance by segregating the leaves from background.
I was also in charge of making the slides and literature review.


We chose a dataset containing 20k images, with 256 by 256 resolution and 15 classes. We had used DenseNet121 for our model. Our model was able to achieve an accuracy of 90% before background removal.


Users can upload images of their leaves to our website, which were linked to the model by flask, and in turn, our website will answer if their plant is diseased.

The following is an example of how the background is segregated. For black backgrounds- all black pixels will be segregated, where as for non black backgrounds, any pixels that are not within a certain HSV value will be segregated.
This allows the model to only focus on the leaves itself and does not create any bias from background, therefore improving the overall performance of the model.

As shown from the image above, there is a way larger variance after segregation, meaning the model was able to learn more without the background.

Outcome

As shown from the overall accuracy, accomplishing my task allowed the model to improve its performance, having 99.99 accuracy and only 1-2 incorrects. Attempting to segregate the background was challenging, I had initially tried to use RGB values, watershed edge detection and K-means to remove the background, but I learnt that HSV values prove to be the best option.
